💡 Today's Concept: THE INTEREST RATE DIFFERENTIAL

An interest rate differential is simply the gap between what two different countries' central banks charge for money. The interest-rate differential between Mexico and the US — roughly 300 basis points (3 percentage points) as of April 2026 — remains a critical anchor for the peso and the carry trade. This means global investors borrow money cheaply in the U.S. and invest it in Mexico to earn higher returns, which boosts demand for pesos and helps keep Mexico's currency strong — but it can reverse quickly if global risks spike.
