💡 Today's Concept: THE PCE INDEX (Personal Consumption Expenditures)

The PCE index measures how much Americans are paying for goods and services over time — it is the Federal Reserve's preferred inflation gauge. 💡 Today's Concept: GDP (Gross Domestic Product)

GDP is the total value of all goods and services a country produces in a given period — think of it as the economy's "report card." When GDP grows, the economy is expanding (more jobs, more income); when it shrinks — as happened in Mexico in Q1 2026 — it signals that businesses and consumers are producing and spending less. GDP growth is typically measured as the annual change in real (inflation-adjusted) GDP compared with the previous year, so it tells you how much the economy truly grew after stripping out the effect of rising prices.
