💡 Today's Concept: THE EARNINGS SURPRISE

What it is: An "earnings surprise" happens when a company reports profits or revenue that are significantly higher (or lower) than what Wall Street analysts expected. The bigger the surprise, the bigger the stock move — because markets had already "priced in" the old forecast.

Next time you hear a company "beat estimates," check by how much: a 5% beat barely moves a stock, while a 65% beat can make history. 💡 Today's Concept: CREDIT RATINGS

What it is: A credit rating is like a report card for a country (or company), issued by agencies like Moody's, S&P, and Fitch, that tells investors how risky it is to lend that country money. A higher rating (like "AAA") means the country is seen as very safe; a lower rating or a "negative outlook" means investors worry the country may struggle to repay its debts.

How to apply it: Moody's recently downgraded Mexico's sovereign rating to "Baa3" — the lowest investment-grade level — and S&P revised Mexico's outlook to "negative." In practice, this means Mexico may have to pay higher interest rates to borrow money internationally, which leaves less money in the federal budget for roads, schools, or social programs. As a beginner investor, whenever you see a credit rating downgrade in the news, ask yourself: "Will the government have less money to spend? 💡 Today's Concept: MARKET CAPITALIZATION vs. VALUATION (PUBLIC vs. PRIVATE)

What it is: Market capitalization is the total value investors assign to a publicly traded company — calculated by multiplying the share price by the number of shares. A "valuation" for a private company like Anthropic works similarly, but instead of a stock price, it's negotiated during a funding round: investors agree to pay a price that implies the whole company is worth a certain amount.

How to apply it: When you read that Anthropic is "valued at $965 billion," treat it like a price tag negotiated in a private deal — not a price set by millions of daily trades on a stock exchange. It means early investors and the company agreed on that number during the latest fundraising round.
